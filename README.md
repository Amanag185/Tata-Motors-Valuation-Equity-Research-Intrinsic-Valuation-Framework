# Tata Motors Valuation: Equity Research & Intrinsic Valuation Framework

A comprehensive equity research and valuation project focused on estimating the intrinsic value of Tata Motors using Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation and Comparable Company Analysis (CCA). The project combines business analysis, financial modeling, industry benchmarking, and sensitivity analysis to develop a data-driven investment thesis.

---

## Overview

Investment decisions should be driven by fundamentals rather than short-term market sentiment. This project evaluates whether Tata Motors is fairly valued by analyzing its financial performance, growth prospects, competitive positioning, and future cash-generating ability.

The valuation framework integrates:

- Equity Research
- Financial Statement Analysis
- Revenue Forecasting
- DCF Valuation
- Comparable Company Analysis (CCA)
- Sensitivity Analysis
- Investment Thesis Development

---

## Objectives

- Evaluate the intrinsic value of Tata Motors using fundamental valuation methodologies.
- Analyze the company’s business model, industry position, and growth drivers.
- Forecast future Free Cash Flows to Firm (FCFF).
- Estimate the appropriate discount rate using WACC.
- Benchmark Tata Motors against listed automotive peers.
- Develop a data-driven investment recommendation.

---

# Company Overview

Tata Motors is one of India's largest automobile manufacturers and a key player in:

- Passenger Vehicles (PV)
- Commercial Vehicles (CV)
- Electric Vehicles (EV)

The company holds:

- ~13% share in the domestic passenger vehicle market
- ~53% share in India's EV market
- ~37.7% share in the domestic commercial vehicle market

The company also owns Jaguar Land Rover (JLR), providing global exposure across premium automotive segments.

# FCFF Modelling

The valuation uses **Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)** as the primary valuation metric.

### FCFF Formula

```text
FCFF = EBIT × (1 – Tax Rate)
       + Depreciation & Amortization
       – Capital Expenditure
       – Change in Net Working Capital
```

### Components Included

- EBIT
- Tax-adjusted earnings (NOPAT)
- Depreciation & Amortization
- Capital Expenditure
- Working Capital Adjustments

The model captures the firm's true cash-generating ability available to all capital providers.

# WACC Estimation

Estimated the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) using market-based inputs.

### Key Inputs

| Parameter | Value |
|------------|--------|
| Risk-Free Rate | 6.5% |
| Equity Risk Premium | 6.0% |
| Beta | 1.2 |
| Cost of Equity | 13.7% |
| Pre-Tax Cost of Debt | 7.5% |
| Tax Rate | 25% |
| Debt Weight | 5% |
| Equity Weight | 95% |
| WACC | ~13.2% |

### Why WACC?

- Captures both business and financing risk
- Provides an appropriate discount rate
- Determines present value of future cash flows

# Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation

DCF valuation was used to estimate the intrinsic value of Tata Motors by discounting future FCFFs using the estimated WACC.

### DCF Process

1. Forecast operating performance
2. Estimate future FCFF
3. Calculate WACC
4. Estimate Terminal Value
5. Discount future cash flows
6. Calculate Enterprise Value
7. Derive Equity Value
8. Estimate Intrinsic Share Price

---

# Terminal Value Estimation

Terminal value captures cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period.

### Perpetuity Growth Formula

```text
Terminal Value =
FCFF × (1 + g)
----------------
(WACC - g)
```

Where:

- g = Terminal Growth Rate
- WACC = Discount Rate

Conservative long-term growth assumptions were used to avoid overvaluation.
